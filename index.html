How to repair that hole in your Drywall

Often times many people encounter a situation where they have a hole in their wall and they need to spend money hiring a handy man for a subpar fix. Those days are now over as I will instruct you on how to properly repair your Drywall!

What you will need

Getting the job done right is going to require purchasing the right tools for the job. I will list everything that is needed below.

  • drywall knives
    • 4 inch knife
    • 6 inch knife
    • 10 inch knife
  • drywall tape
  • joint compound
  • joint compound pan
  • utility knife
  • drywall sanding sponge
  • drywall patch
  • spare wood for backing

Instructions Part 1: Putting in the patch piece

  1. First, this may sound counter-intuitive but you will need to enlarge the hole in the wall by marking the shape of a square around the existing hole.

  2. Next, you will cut a square piece of drywall with the same dimensions as the cutout you made in the wall. this cutout will serve as the patch to repair the hole in the wall.

  3. Next, you will than want to take 2 pieces of wood which will serve as the support backing for you patch piece and cut them long enough so that it extends past the edges of the whole atleast 4-5 inches

  4. Next, you will gather atleast 8 drywall screws and use them to secure the backing wood to both sides of the patch opening right at the edge so that it is visible like a border. This will serve as the backing that will enable you to secure your patch to the wall.

  5. Now, you will gather another 8 drywall screws and secure your patch to the wall

  6. After your patch piece is secured to the wall, that will complete phase one of the repair. However, you will quickly realize that securing the patch piece still doenst look like a finished job. In order to attain a proper finished look, it will require what tradesman call "taping and mudding".

Instructions Part 2:Taping and Mudding

It is not enough to just screw drywall to studs or secure a drywall patch to backing wood on the wall. Doing so leaves a project looking unfinished. To have that finished look, all the joints and nails need to be covered up so that the wall can have a continuos and seamless look.

  1. First, you may need to prefill the joints of your patch depending on how well that patch fits the hole that you created. To prefill a joint is to simply apply joint compound to joint that has a gap to large to apply joint tape over it. The goal is not perfection in this step, rather, you simply want to fill in that gap without applying to much material.

  2. Next, cut the mesh tape into the proper length for each side of your patch, you will then seat the tape directly over each joint.

  3. Now, take your 4 inch knife and apply a thin coat of joint compound over the patch to begin covering the tape. Be sure to keep this coat thin and feather the edges.

  4. Next, you will use your 6 inch knife to apply a second coat. You will want to achieve more coverage and width with this coat. Again, be sure to feather your edges!

  5. For your final coat you will now utilize your 10 inch knife. This coat should be the final pass making everything nice, smooth, and ready to sand. Using the 10 inch blade ensures that you spread the patch even wider creating a seemingly invisible joint.

  6. The final step is to use your sanding sponge to remove any imperfections such as small spots that were not feathered and/or lines or lift offs created by the knife.

  7. Now you should have a smooth patch with invisible joints ready for prime and paint!

Instructions Part 3:Prime & Paint

After your patch is sanded and dusted off, you are now ready for priming and painting to complete the project.

  1. First use a paint roller to apply drywall primer over the entire patch area. Apply 1-2 coats. Primer ensures an even coverage of paint preventing the patch area from flashing through the paint as a result of painting over bare joint compound.

  2. Finally you can apply 1-2 coats of paint with a roller to complete the project.
