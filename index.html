How to repair that hole in your Drywall

Often times many people encounter a situation where they have a hole in their wall and they need to spend money hiring a handy man for a subpar fix. Those days are now over as I will instruct you on how to properly repair your Drywall!

What you will need

Getting the job done right is going to require purchasing the right tools for the job. I will list everything that is needed below.

  • drywall knives
    • 4 inch knife
    • 6 inch knife
    • 10 inch knife
  • drywall tape
  • joint compound
  • joint compound pan
  • utility knife
  • drywall sanding sponge
  • drywall patch
  • spare wood for backing

Instructions Part 1: Putting in the patch piece

  1. First, this may sound counter-intuitive but you will need to enlarge the hole in the wall by marking the shape of a square around the existing hole.

  2. Next, you will cut a square piece of drywall with the same dimensions as the cutout you made in the wall. this cutout will serve as the patch to repair the hole in the wall.

  3. Next, you will than want to take 2 pieces of wood which will serve as the support backing for you patch piece and cut them long enough so that it extends past the edges of the whole atleast 4-5 inches

  4. Next, you will gather atleast 8 drywall screws and use them to secure the backing wood to both sides of the patch opening right at the edge so that it is visible like a border. This will serve as the backing that will enable you to secure your patch to the wall.

  5. Now, you will gather another 8 drywall screws and secure your patch to the wall

  6. After your patch piece is secured to the wall, that will complete phase one of the repair. However, you will quickly realize that securing the patch piece still doenst look like a finished job. In order to attain a proper finished look, it will require what tradesman call "taping and mudding"./p>

Instructions Part 2:Taping and Mudding

It is not enough to just screw drywall to studs or secure a drywall patch to backing wood on the wall. Doing so leaves a project looking unfinished. To have that finished look, all the joints and nails need to be covered up so that the wall can have a continuos and seamless look.
